Our Story

How it all began

Some love stories begin with a glance across a crowded room. Others start with a system error, a surprise meeting, and a woman with glasses who made a man forget everything he was supposed to be doing. Ours is a story of laughter, distance, adventure, and the quiet certainty that this is the person I want by my side — through every chapter, in every season.

From Querétaro to Minneapolis and everywhere in between, Estefania and Adam found in each other a home — one that has nothing to do with geography and everything to do with belonging.

Our Story

The moments that brought us here

July 1st, 2024

How We Met

It started with a system error and a meeting neither of them expected. On July 1st, 2024, Fannie's team discovered an issue in the Orders system Adam managed — which meant Fannie, her bosses, and a very nervous Adam were suddenly in a room together. Adam was focused on the problem. Mostly. Mostly he was distracted by the curious, glasses-wearing woman across the table who had just upended his afternoon and made him forget — briefly — everything about the issue he was supposed to be fixing.

August 1st, 2024

Our Unofficial First Date

A follow-up meeting was called to produce a summary document explaining the issue to leadership. On August 1st, 2024, Fannie and Adam sat down with the best of intentions and produced approximately zero words of it. What they did produce was three hours of conversation about their lives, their interests, and whatever that fluttery thing was happening between them. The document could wait. This, apparently, could not.

September 21st, 2024

Our First Date

After weeks of messaging daily, they finally scheduled the real thing — September 21st, 2024. Adam flew to Querétaro and they went to Cervecería Hércules, a beloved local brewery in the heart of Santiago de Querétaro. Pizza, a couple of beers to calm the nerves, and two people whose faces hurt by the end of the night from smiling and laughing too much. There may have been some kissing too.

September 13th, 2025

The Proposal

Every trip Adam and Fannie take together is meticulously planned — they like to make the most of their time. So when Fannie came to visit for their one-year anniversary and one Saturday was left open, Adam volunteered to plan it. Fannie did not suspect a thing. Or so we're told.

The day was perfect — breakfast in bed, a drive to Stillwater, a beautiful lunch along the river, ice cream, and a lift bridge crossing. On the drive home, Fannie told Adam it was a perfect day and that he should always plan their time together. He responded, jokingly, that he still had time to mess it up. Neither of them knew how right he was.

Back home they put together a charcuterie board and headed to Father Hennepin Bluff Park. While packing it up, Adam quietly reached into his backpack for the ring box. Then he excused himself to check the ring was set and ready.

He opened the box.

It was empty.

The ring was at home. Very much at home. After what felt like a small eternity in a park bathroom, Adam walked out, sat down next to Fannie, and said he needed a moment. He decided to go through with it anyway.

They walked across the Stone Arch Bridge as the city glowed around them. Fannie looked beautiful in her white dress. He finally found the right moment and said:

"Fannie, will you marry me? I know there isn't a ring in the box — but I have it back home."

She looked at him and said:

"Yeah. Yeah, I don't care."

"So that's a yes?"

"YES."

They hugged, laughed, and took pictures on the bridge. An older gentleman passing asked what she said. Adam told him she said yes. They raced home, grabbed the actual ring, and slipped it on her finger — just in time for Tom, Adam's roommate, to witness the moment on his way in. He was the first person to know they were engaged, and the first to hear the story of the empty box.

Then they raced back downtown to celebrate over dinner at Mara Restaurant and Bar.

Meet the Couple

Two lives, one love story

E

Estefania

Querétaro born and raised, Estefania brings warmth, creativity, and an infectious joy to every room she enters. She's deeply faithful, fiercely driven, and somehow also the person who will hand-draw you an illustrated recap of your entire year together as an anniversary gift. She painted Adam his favorite national park — Zion.

When she's not creating something beautiful — paintings, pottery, photo books, or elaborate anniversary illustrations — she's taking excellent care of Bruno, her dog, who has strong opinions about most things. She sees her family every Sunday at church and lunch, a tradition that says everything about who she is.

She is also, for the record, a devoted Harry Potter fan. The sorting hat knows what it did.

A

Adam

A Minnesota native with a wanderer's heart, Adam is equally at home paddling a canoe through the Boundary Waters, hiking a mountain out west, or cheering on the Minnesota Vikings from his season tickets — which he has held, through thick and thin, since 2013. Mostly thin.

He maintains a media server, works out at his CrossFit gym with a crew of close friends, and takes Nova on daily walks — which she demands and deserves.

Adam works hard at what he does, shows up for the people he loves, and has a remarkable ability to stay calm under pressure. Except, apparently, when packing engagement rings.
