Venue & Location

The Venue

Fiesta Americana Hacienda Galindo Resort & Spa
Carretera San Juan del Río a Amealco, km. 5,
San Juan del Río, Querétaro, Mexico

A stunning colonial hacienda dating back to the 16th century, now a luxury resort surrounded by lush gardens, fountains, and the timeless beauty of the Querétaro highlands.

Where exactly is Hacienda Galindo?

+

Hacienda Galindo is located in San Juan del Río, in the state of Querétaro, Mexico — about 1.5 hours north of Mexico City and 1 hour south of the city of Querétaro. The surrounding countryside is beautiful and the hacienda itself is set among gardens and colonial architecture.

Will the ceremony be indoors or outdoors?

+

The ceremony will take place outside by the vineyard. The cocktail hour will be held in the outdoor garden courtyard, and the reception dinner and dancing will be outside next to the vineyard as well. We recommend bringing a light wrap for the cooler evening air.

Travel & Getting There

What is the closest airport?

+

We recommend flying into Querétaro Intercontinental Airport (QRO) — it is approximately 1 hour from the venue. We will have shuttles running from QRO to Hacienda Galindo the day before the wedding, and from Hacienda Galindo back to QRO the day after, so you can arrive and depart stress-free.

How do I get from the airport to the venue?

+

Several options:

  • Rental car: Recommended for flexibility. Highway 57D connects Mexico City and Querétaro and is a smooth, well-maintained toll road.
  • Shuttle service: The hotel can arrange private shuttle transfers from both airports. Contact the resort directly for rates.
  • Bus: ETN and Primera Plus run luxury buses from Mexico City's North Terminal (Terminal del Norte) to San Juan del Río. Comfortable and affordable.
  • Rideshare/Taxi: Available from both airports — agree on a price before you go if taking a regular taxi.

Do I need a visa to travel to Mexico?

+

Citizens of the United States, Canada, and most European countries do not need a visa for tourism stays up to 180 days. However, requirements vary by nationality — please check with your country's travel authority or the Mexican consulate website well in advance of your trip.

All visitors will need to fill out an FMM (tourist permit) upon entry, which is typically handled at the airport or border crossing.

Is Querétaro safe to visit?

+

Querétaro is consistently ranked one of the safest states in Mexico and is a popular destination for both domestic and international travelers. San Juan del Río is a peaceful, charming town. As always, exercise common sense while traveling — keep an eye on your belongings and use established transportation options.

Miscellaneous

What is the weather like in Querétaro in fall?

+

Fall in Querétaro is lovely — typically warm days (around 20–25°C / 68–77°F) and cool evenings (12–16°C / 54–61°F). October is at the tail end of the rainy season, so expect mostly sunny days with an occasional light shower. We recommend packing a light jacket for the evenings.

Can I explore the area while I'm there?

+

Absolutely — we encourage it! Querétaro is a UNESCO World Heritage city with a stunning colonial historic center. Nearby highlights include:

  • The city of Querétaro — aqueduct, Templo de Santa Rosa de Viterbo, plazas
  • Tequisquiapan — charming town known for wine and cheese
  • Wine Route of Querétaro — several boutique wineries within an hour's drive
  • San Miguel de Allende — about 1.5 hours away, a must-visit colonial gem
